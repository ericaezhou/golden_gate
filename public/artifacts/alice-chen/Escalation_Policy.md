## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes the escalation framework for credit loss reserve adjustments and related risk management decisions.

## 2. Scope

Applies to all manual adjustments to model-generated loss forecasts across consumer credit portfolios.

## 3. Definitions

- **Overlay:** A manual adjustment to model output based on analyst judgment
- **Material Adjustment:** Any adjustment impacting reserves by more than $500K
- **Model Staleness:** When underlying model has not been recalibrated for >30 days

## 4. Escalation Thresholds

### 4.1 Routine Adjustments (<$2M impact)

- Analyst may apply adjustment with documentation
- Quarterly review by Risk Committee
- No pre-approval required

### 4.2 Significant Adjustments ($2M - $5M impact)

- Requires Risk Committee alignment before implementation
- Must be discussed at weekly Risk Committee sync
- Documentation required in adjustment log

### 4.3 Major Adjustments (>$5M impact)

- Requires CFO formal approval via email
- Risk Committee must be informed in advance
- For adjustments exceeding $5M impact, **see Alice Chen for the specific approval workflow**
- Board notification required for adjustments >$10M

## 5. Documentation Requirements

### 5.1 All Adjustments Must Include:

- Date of adjustment
- Segment(s) affected
- Dollar amount and percentage impact
- Rationale with supporting data
- Approver name (where required)

### 5.2 Supporting Analysis

Adjustments should reference:
- Cohort performance data
- Macroeconomic indicators
- Early delinquency trends
- Any relevant external factors

## 6. Overlay Removal

### 6.1 Review Cadence

All active overlays must be reviewed quarterly to assess whether triggering conditions persist.

### 6.2 Removal Criteria

An overlay should be removed when:
- Triggering condition has normalized for 2+ consecutive quarters
- Model has been recalibrated to capture the pattern
- Risk Committee approves removal

### 6.3 Approval for Removal

- Removal of overlays >$2M requires same approval as original implementation
- CFO sign-off required for removal of overlays >$5M

## 7. Model Staleness Protocol

When model exceeds 30-day staleness threshold:

1. Notify Risk Committee at next sync
2. Document any adjustments with "stale model" flag
3. Prioritize recalibration in model development queue

## 8. Emergency Procedures

For rapidly deteriorating conditions requiring immediate adjustment:

1. Analyst prepares emergency memo
2. CRO or CFO verbal approval
3. Formal documentation within 24 hours
4. Risk Committee notification at next meeting
